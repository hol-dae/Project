Climate-Resilient Seed Varieties

Discover crop seed varieties designed to thrive under extreme weather conditions — drought, floods, heat, and salinity.

Explore Seed Varieties

What Are Climate-Resilient Seeds?

Climate-resilient seeds are specially bred or genetically selected to survive extreme environmental conditions like drought, flooding, high temperatures, and soil salinity. These seeds ensure stable yields even under adverse climatic changes — helping farmers maintain productivity while adapting to global warming.

Key Benefits

  • Drought Tolerance: Survive with minimal water during dry spells.
  • Flood Resistance: Grow well in waterlogged fields and heavy rain zones.
  • Heat Adaptation: Maturity cycles match changing temperature patterns.
  • Salinity Tolerance: Grow in salt-affected soils due to sea-level rise or irrigation damage.
  • Higher Yields: Maintain productivity despite erratic weather.
  • Improved Food Security: Ensure harvest stability in vulnerable regions.

Drought-Tolerant Maize

Ideal for semi-arid areas where rainfall is unpredictable and water scarcity is common.

Developed by CIMMYT, these maize hybrids help African farmers grow crops with up to 40% less water than traditional varieties. Farmers report better germination rates and higher grain quality even during prolonged dry periods.

Flood-Tolerant Rice

These rice strains can survive submersion for up to two weeks without loss of viability.

Known as “Scuba Rice,” this variety has been introduced across Bangladesh, India, and parts of Southeast Asia. It allows farmers to recover quickly after monsoon floods.

Heat-Resistant Wheat

Matures faster before peak summer heat impacts grain development and yield.

ICAR and IRRI have developed wheat lines that mature quickly before the worst heatwaves hit. Ideal for Central Asia and North Africa.

Salt-Tolerant Barley

Thrives in coastal and arid regions affected by rising salinity levels in soil and water.

Used in Middle Eastern countries and coastal regions in South Asia, these barley strains resist salt buildup in soil and allow year-round cultivation.

Disease-Resistant Cassava

Genetically improved cassava resists whitefly infestation and brown streak virus affecting East African farms.

The International Institute of Tropical Agriculture (IITA) has introduced disease-resistant cassava in Uganda, Kenya, and Tanzania, boosting food security and reducing losses.

Heat-Tolerant Sorghum

Performs well in hot, semi-arid regions and supports livestock feed and human consumption.

ICRISAT’s heat-tolerant sorghum strains are now being used in Ethiopia, Sudan, and parts of India to sustain yields during rising temperatures.

Climate-Resilient Beans

Bean varieties that resist pests and diseases caused by shifting climate patterns and rising humidity.

CIAT and NARO Uganda have developed bean strains that mature faster and resist rust and wilt under erratic rainfall and high humidity.

Salinity-Tolerant Tomatoes

Grow in saline soils where regular tomatoes fail due to salt stress and low water availability.

Used in Egypt, Philippines, and coastal India, these tomato plants withstand salt intrusion and deliver reliable yields even in degraded lands.

Leading Institutions in Seed Innovation

  • CIMMYT – Mexico: Breeding drought-tolerant maize and wheat for smallholder farms globally.
  • IRRI – Philippines: Developing flood-tolerant rice and training local agronomists in climate-adaptive farming techniques.
  • IITA – Nigeria: Improving cassava and yam resistance to pests and viruses through biotech and farmer-led breeding programs.
  • ICAR – India: Introducing heat-tolerant pulses and millets for arid and semi-arid climates.
  • World Bank: Funding large-scale resilient seed distribution in climate-vulnerable regions.

Real-Life Applications

Zambia - Drought-Tolerant Maize

Over 10,000 smallholder farmers adopted new hybrid maize strains, increasing average yield by 25% during dry seasons.

This project was supported by the Zambian Ministry of Agriculture and international seed banks to improve food access in Southern Africa.

Bangladesh - Flood-Tolerant Rice

Submersion-tolerant rice varieties helped farmers recover faster after monsoon floods in southern Bangladesh.

The Scuba Rice program expanded to Nepal and Myanmar, providing flood-prone communities with life-saving agricultural tools.
