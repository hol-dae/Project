Real-Life Use Cases

In parts of Kenya, local cooperatives have started adopting AI-powered yield tools that integrate with mobile apps. These tools help smallholder farmers anticipate their maize production months in advance.

By knowing expected output, farmers were able to secure contracts with grain buyers and plan storage facilities more effectively, increasing income and reducing post-harvest losses.

How the Prediction Works

This simplified AI model uses linear regression logic based on known farming datasets. The formula used is: 

Yield = Base_Yield + (Rainfall × 0.02) + (Moisture × 0.05) + (Temp_Factor × 1.5) + (Fertilizer × 0.03)
  • Rainfall: Higher rainfall increases yield up to an optimal point.
  • Moisture: Maintains soil hydration and affects nutrient uptake.
  • Temperature: Too low or too high reduces growth; moderate is best.
  • Fertilizer: Increases yield proportionally up to a limit.
